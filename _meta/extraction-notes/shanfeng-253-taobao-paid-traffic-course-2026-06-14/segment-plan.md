## Must-Keep Anchors

- Multi-link launch for one confirmed product or patent product.
- Same product spread by scenario, crowd, material, color, and link.
- Early objective is transactions and audience labels, not perfect ROI.
- Plan purpose must differ: acquisition, transaction, conversion control, harvest.
- Search traffic share may cap at around 20%-30% in some categories.
- Review plan efficiency and move budget away from high-spend low-ROI plans.
- Competitor traffic price and plan allocation should be benchmarked before pushing.
- Test audience and high-click material early; otherwise later scaling wastes cycles.
- Front-end acquisition without harvest risks seeding for competitors.
- All-site promotion includes search, information flow, and off-site recommendation in the speaker's framing.
- Use same-category benchmark conversion/ROI to choose keyword vs crowd route.
- Do not brush orders casually because labels may become messy.
- Start all-site when transaction attributes, expected buyer profile, or platform recommendation signals appear.
- Budget size changes audience quality and speed; same item with different budgets can behave differently.
- Switch from maximize to target ROI only after enough transaction labels.
- Raise target ROI gradually, e.g. by about 0.2-0.4 depending on category.
- Observe 8-24 hours after increases before adding too much.
- If conversion is low, fix product foundation, page/material, or audience mismatch before adding budget.
- If product total market volume is small, unlimited budget cannot create enough traffic.
- Do not frequently switch plans back and forth after the system has learned.
- Short-video content can help acquisition/seeding once all-site is stable.
- Video assessment emphasizes first three seconds, click entry, and subsequent add-cart/conversion.
- Use similar stores, own push item, category behaviors, keywords, platform recommendation, and buyer exclusion rules.
- Final workflow is market -> product -> promotion/marketing -> repeatable explosive-product pipeline.
- AI/COC is positioned as support for demand extraction, review analysis, detail-page planning, and visual/material generation.
