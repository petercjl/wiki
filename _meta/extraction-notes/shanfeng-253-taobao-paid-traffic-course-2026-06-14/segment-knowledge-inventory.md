# 山峰组253期6-14课程 knowledge inventory

## Core Claims

1. A promising product should be given enough traffic opportunities before being judged; one link is often not enough.
2. In early launch, transaction labels are more important than perfect ROI, because the system needs evidence about who buys.
3. Paid traffic plans should have different jobs: initial acquisition, transaction acceleration, audience correction, and harvest.
4. All-site promotion should not be operated blindly; it needs candidate-product signals, benchmark data, and staged objectives.
5. Budget determines both speed and audience quality; a higher budget is not merely "more of the same".
6. Switching from maximize volume to target ROI should happen after transaction labels and buyer profile become clearer.
7. ROI target should be raised gradually; sudden high targets or frequent switching can damage learned matching.
8. If product/page/material conversion is weak, adding budget amplifies waste rather than fixing the root cause.
9. If market traffic volume is too small, paid promotion cannot create a large business by force.
10. Short-video/content placement is not a replacement for all-site; it is an acquisition/seeding supplement after stable validation.
11. Acquisition without harvest risks handing seeded users to competitors.
12. The final operating system is not a single product push, but a repeatable pipeline from market decision to product, visual/material, promotion, and A/B/C product ladder.

## Decision Rules

- If product certainty is high but one link has not risen, create multiple scene/crowd/material links rather than judging the product too early.
- If the current goal is labeling and transaction evidence, tolerate some ROI imperfection and crowd deviation.
- If a plan spends much but has poor conversion/ROI, shrink the plan and move budget to better-performing plans.
- If the product has transaction attributes, expected buyer profile, platform recommendation, or a clear trend, all-site can be tested.
- If no transaction labels exist, do not rush into target ROI mode.
- If target ROI is below final profit requirement, raise by small steps and observe before the next increase.
- If the plan has learned a stable pattern, avoid frequent back-and-forth switching.
- If all-site has reached a stable ceiling, add short-video/content seeding to expand the upper funnel.

## Numeric Anchors

- Search traffic share mentioned as often around 20%, sometimes 30% when conditions are weak.
- Initial plan limits/examples include 50-100, 100, 200, 300, 500, 1000, 1500, 2000 budgets depending on stage and category.
- ROI target examples include break-even 3, first target 1.5 or 2, then gradual increases by 0.2-0.4.
- Observation window after budget/target increase: 8-24 hours.
- Short-video/content assessment mentions first three seconds.
- Case anchors include spend from 1000-3000/3800 and later daily spend around 15000-20000 in the speaker's example.

## Case Anchors

- Patent/confirmed product that failed to rise with only one link: illustrates need for more scenarios and links.
- Existing product/category extension with about 20 styles: illustrates scenario/crowd expansion without many new base products.
- Tight/standardized categories and laser/tightener examples: illustrate category fit and bid/crowd premium uncertainty.
- Handbag example: illustrates ROI/ROAS target climbing, budget expansion, and needing to adjust target when value rises.
- Colors/specs/multiple links under one SPU: illustrates small-budget link split for additional sales support.
- Short-video example: illustrates testing content material, first three seconds, click/add-cart/conversion, then replicating with controlled-cost plans.
